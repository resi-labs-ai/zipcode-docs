# Legal Structuring

Every lien is held in a **bankruptcy-remote SPV** — insulated from both the originator's corporate credit and from Zipcode's own operating entity. An originator bankruptcy does not sweep the lien; a wind-down of Zipcode's operating entity does not touch it either.

### Custody

The SPV holds the lien directly. The originator has no claim on it beyond the terms of their repurchase agreement, and cannot re-pledge or withdraw it early.

### Repurchase

Each HELOC originator signs a **repurchase agreement** at draw: they must repurchase the lien and repay the loan within a fixed term (90 days). If they don't, the SPV sells the lien through the secondary market relationship the originator was underwritten against — proceeds return to depositors ahead of any originator claim.

### Eligibility

Access to a Credit Line requires qualification on two levels — the originator (KYB, existing secondary market relationships) and the specific lien (value, and the qualification standards of the secondary market partner who will take it out).
