# Risk

### 1. Secondary Market Shutdown
Our lines of credit are underwritten by the preferences of specific take-out providers / secondary markets, before the loan is even offered. However, in 2008, secondary markets for HELOC positions were broadely shut down for a time, cutting the buy pressure from the market, and bankrupting Credit Warehouses who were reliant on those capital flows. 

They got stuck holding HELOC liens, which are 2nd lien agreements, during a housing downturn, with no secondary market -- and those liens received haircuts on the underlying paper, because in settlement, 2nd lien agreements do not take precedent over 1st lien agreements. 

* A sudden 2008 style scenario would cause an extended duration freeze on szipUSD shares.
* Some people would leave, accepting haircuts through the CoW orderbook.
* Yield would have to be directed to plug holes. 
* The protocol would need to decide whether to sell the lien at a loss, or take custody and wait until the secondary market recovered.
* The protocol would have difficulty maintaining operations because cashflow and reserves would likely need to be directed toward buying szipUSD shares. 

### 2. Limited Liquidity Run
In a situation where some default or freeze is taking place, and liquidity is limited, impatient depositors will likely capitulate into unfavorable CoW bids. 

### 3. Key Compromise
If the team is irresponsible with its key management, a change could be made in the timelock, which would start a 2-day self-destruct process on the effected silos. 

* All liquid value for that Silo would need to be exited through CoW redemptions. 
* Creditors would be contacted.
* A Recovery Silo could be deployed.

Value capture could be minimized.

### 4. Governance / build-phase wiring.
Until the pre-production immutable re-freeze, the Timelock can re-point wiring. A single one-sided re-point fails closed (a real redirect needs two coordinated Timelock actions on two contracts), and the owner has no sweep, pause, or NAV-inflation power. The decisive warehouse control — the Zodiac Roles scope — lives in deploy config by design, and that deployed scope tree is the single most important audit artifact. See [Roles & Permissions](/reference/control).
